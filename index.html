Proof of Consent for Toll-Free SMS Verification

Application: Brav Buddies Emergency Mobilization System

Consent Mechanism

Our application obtains explicit consent from users before sending any SMS messages. Below is the documented consent flow:

1. User Registration & Phone Number Collection

When users (firefighters, soldiers, or emergency personnel) are added to the system, their phone numbers are collected and stored by authorized personnel (commanders or platoon leaders) with the understanding that these numbers will be used for emergency mobilization communications.

Database Schema Evidence:

  • Table: platoon_members
  • Field: phone_number (stored with user profile)
  • Purpose: Emergency contact and mobilization notifications

2. Explicit Consent Language

Users are informed through organizational onboarding that by providing their phone number to the Emergency Mobilization System, they are:

Consenting to receive time-sensitive emergency mobilization alerts
Agreeing to receive training completion reminders
Understanding that messages are critical for mission readiness
Aware they can respond to messages to confirm availability

Message Types:

  • Emergency mission activation notifications
  • Training status reminders
  • Response acknowledgment confirmations

3. Opt-In Process

Phone numbers are only added to the system when:

  1. Organizational Authorization: Unit commanders or leaders explicitly add personnel
  2. Official Capacity: Users are part of an official emergency response organization
  3. Emergency Purpose: Communications are exclusively for emergency mobilization and training readiness

This is NOT marketing communication - all messages are operational and time-critical.

4. Sample Message Content

Mission Activation:

MISSION ALERT: [Mission Name]
Location: [Address]
Report: [Date/Time]
Reply: AVAILABLE or UNAVAILABLE
- Emergency Mobilization System

Training Reminder:

Training Update: [Course Name]
Status: Incomplete
Complete by: [Date]
Contact your leader for access.
- Emergency Mobilization System

5. Opt-Out Mechanism

Users can opt-out by:

  • Contacting their unit commander/leader to remove their phone number
  • Replying "STOP" to any message (standard SMS opt-out)
  • Requesting removal through their chain of command

OPT Out Messaging:

Reply STOP to opt out, HELP for assistance. Msg & data rates may apply.

6. Data Privacy & Security

  • Phone numbers are stored securely in an encrypted database
  • Access restricted to authorized organizational leaders
  • Used exclusively for emergency mobilization purposes
  • Compliant with organizational data handling policies

7. Use Case Justification

Emergency Services & Public Safety:

This application serves emergency response organizations (fire departments, military reserve units, emergency medical services) where rapid mobilization is critical for public safety. SMS notifications are essential for:

  • Time-sensitive mission activations
  • Personnel accountability during emergencies
  • Ensuring trained personnel respond to critical incidents
  • Maintaining operational readiness

Verification Contact

  • Organization Type: Emergency Response / Public Safety
  • Use Case: Emergency Mobilization & Training Readiness
  • Consent Method: Organizational enrollment with explicit purpose disclosure
  • Message Frequency: Variable (based on emergency activations and training status)
  • Opt-Out: Available via chain of command or standard SMS STOP

Declaration:

All users added to this system are informed of SMS communications as part of their emergency response duties. Phone numbers are provided with full knowledge and consent for emergency mobilization purposes only.
